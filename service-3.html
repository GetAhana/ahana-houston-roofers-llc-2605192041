When to call us

After a hail or wind storm, look for these five signs that your roof may have damage requiring professional inspection.

  • Dents or bruises on shingles (often 1–2 inches across) indicate impact damage that may not immediately leak but weakens the roof.
  • Missing shingles or exposed underlayment create obvious vulnerability to rain and accelerated wear.
  • Granule loss or bald spots on shingles after a storm signal impact damage even if the structure seems intact.
  • Damaged or bent gutters, downspouts, or fascia suggest wind force strong enough to damage the roof above.
  • Leaks that start after a storm (even days later) often trace to impact-weakened flashing or vents.

FAQ — Storm damage

Do you handle emergency storm tarping?

Yes—we provide same-day or next-day tarping and temporary stabilization for active leaks. We document the emergency work with photos and schedule the permanent repair for the following week when your adjuster has time to inspect.

How do you document storm damage for insurance?

We inspect for hail impact dents (often 1–2 inches across), bruised shingles, missing granules, and broken flashing. We photograph damage patterns and provide a detailed report your insurance adjuster can reference during their inspection.

How do you help with the repair-or-replace decision?

We explain repair vs. replacement based on damage extent, age, and insurance estimates. We never pressure you toward the most expensive option—sometimes a targeted repair costs less and makes more sense. Your insurance adjuster and your budget guide the decision.
